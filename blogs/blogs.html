Lesson's Learned - Phishing scams.

Published on: 1/26/2024

The other day, I encountered my first phishing scam since starting my cybersecurity career. Can you guess what I did? I must admit, I'm quite embarrassed. It took me about 5 minutes, including CLICKING on the link, reviewing the fake website, and contemplating entering card information, to recognize and realize it was a phishing scam. The scam was particularly deceptive, mimicking a USPS notification about a package delivery failure. Since I was expecting a package that day, I didn't initially question it. Looking back, there were numerous indicators of the phishing scam, from the sender's name to the peculiar wording in the message, along with unusual instructions. Despite having recently obtained a couple of certifications, I fell for a phishing test. This incident made me realize the danger of pride, which can cloud judgment and diminish awareness. Those crafting these scams are smarter than we might think; it's never wise to underestimate the enemy. I've taken steps to change my passwords and continue using a password manager. This experience serves as a valuable wakeup call, emphasizing the need for constant vigilance and humility in the face of evolving cyber threats. It's a lesson worth sharing, as cybersecurity is dynamic, and learning from such experiences is essential for personal and professional growth.
